How to Ace Your OAU Post-UTME: Proven Strategies for 2026

Admin Scholar
June 19, 2026
4 min read

Preparing for the Obafemi Awolowo University (OAU) Post-UTME can be a daunting experience. With thousands of students competing for limited admission slots, having a strategy is what separates the successful candidates from the rest. The key is not just working hard, but working smart.

1. Master the Past Questions

OAU is famous for testing your speed, accuracy, and foundational knowledge. A well-known secret among scholars is that the university heavily relies on concepts tested in previous years. By immersing yourself in ExamGuard's practice portal, you are essentially training your brain to recognize patterns in the way questions are structured.

Advertisement

2. Time Management is Everything

During the exam, you will have less than a minute to answer each question. Do not spend 5 minutes trying to solve a complex physics calculation when there are 10 easy biology questions waiting for you. Learn the art of skipping. Answer the ones you know immediately, then return to the challenging ones later.

"The difference between an average score and an excellent score in OAU Post-UTME is almost always time management. Practice under strict exam conditions."

3. Focus on Your Weaknesses Early

It's natural to want to study the subjects you are already good at because it feels rewarding. However, your biggest score improvements will come from turning your weak subjects into average ones. Dedicate your morning study sessions to the topics you dread the most while your mind is still fresh.

4. Stay Updated and Rested

In the final week before your exam, avoid cramming new, complex formulas. Instead, focus on revision and mock tests. More importantly, ensure you get at least 7 hours of sleep the night before your exam. A sharp, rested brain will outperform a tired, crammed brain every single time.
