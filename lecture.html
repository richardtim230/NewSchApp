1. Introduction

A differential equation is an equation involving an unknown function and its derivatives. They model real-world phenomena like population growth, radioactive decay, and mechanics.

Ordinary vs Partial

ODEs have one independent variable (usually $t$ or $x$), while PDEs have multiple (e.g., heat equation).

2. First-Order ODEs

General form:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = Q(x)$$

Separable Equations

Example:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = xy \quad \Rightarrow \quad \int \frac{dy}{y} = \int x\, dx$$ $$ \ln|y| = \frac{x^2}{2} + C \quad \Rightarrow \quad y = Ae^{x^2/2} $$

3. Second-Order Linear ODEs

Homogeneous with constant coefficients:

$$ay'' + by' + cy = 0$$

Characteristic Equation

Roots General Solution
Real distinct $r_1, r_2$$y = c_1 e^{r_1 x} + c_2 e^{r_2 x}$
Repeated $r$$y = (c_1 + c_2 x)e^{rx}$
Complex $\alpha \pm \beta i$$y = e^{\alpha x}(c_1 \cos \beta x + c_2 \sin \beta x)$

4. Laplace Transforms

Transforms ODEs into algebraic equations:

$$\mathcal{L}\{y''\} = s^2 Y(s) - s y(0) - y'(0)$$ 

import sympy as sp
s, t = sp.symbols('s t')
Y = sp.laplace_transform(sp.exp(-2*t)*sp.sin(3*t), t, s)
print(Y)

5. Systems of Differential Equations

Predator-Prey (Lotka-Volterra):

$$\frac{dx}{dt} = \alpha x - \beta xy, \quad \frac{dy}{dt} = \delta xy - \gamma y$$
