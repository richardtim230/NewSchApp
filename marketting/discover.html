Life & Boarding

A warm home, outstanding care, and a world of opportunity

Our boarding environment puts wellbeing and growth at the centre. Residential tutors, houseparents and dedicated pastoral leads work with teachers and counsellors to provide a consistent, supportive experience that complements academic life and fosters independence.

Students enjoy structured academic mentoring, evening study support, weekend enrichment and regular whole-house events — all designed to build belonging, resilience and leadership. Meals are family-style, houses are mixed by age and interest, and senior students have mentorship roles that develop responsibility.

Our houses — community, tradition, care

Six historic boarding houses — each with its own character — create the foundations for strong pastoral care and lifelong friendships. Houses are overseen by houseparents and supported by tutors and dedicated resident tutors.

  • Westbrook House — Known for its academic mentorship programmes and small-group tutorials.
  • Harrington House — Strong emphasis on music, arts and cultural activities, with dedicated practice rooms.
  • Pemberton House — Leadership-focused house with extensive community service links and student committees.
  • Ridgeway House — Vibrant house with an active sports programme and weekend outdoor trips.
  • Elmwell House — Cozy pastoral environment for younger boarders with age-appropriate routines and support.
  • Oakfield House — Senior house offering mentorship roles, independent study spaces and university guidance clinics.

Every new joiner receives a tailored induction, a house buddy for the first term and a clear pastoral plan developed with parents.

A typical boarding week

Our routines balance structure with flexibility so students can pursue academics, hobbies and rest.

Weekday (Mon–Fri)
  • 07:30 — Breakfast & morning briefing
  • 08:30–15:30 — Lessons (with supervised break times)
  • 16:00 — Sports / clubs / music lessons
  • 18:00 — House dinner (family-style)
  • 19:30–21:00 — Evening study & tutor check-ins
  • 21:30 — Lights out (varies by age)
Weekend
  • Saturday — Enrichment workshops, trips, inter-house competitions
  • Sunday — Community service, study skills sessions, family time
  • Regular Parent & Family weekends through the year

Health & safety

We maintain rigorous safeguarding policies, secure accommodation and routine safety drills (fire, first-aid). All staff undergo DBS checks and regular training.

  • Secure campus with controlled entry
  • 24/7 nursing & first-aid
  • Regular risk assessments for trips and activities

Frequently asked questions

How are students matched to houses?
We consider age, interests and pastoral needs to create balanced houses. Parents can express preferences at application time.
Can day students access boarding facilities?
Day students may join after-school clubs and weekend activities but overnight boarding is reserved for full boarders.
What support is available for international students?
We provide airport transfers, visa guidance, dedicated international pastoral staff and an induction programme tailored to newcomers.
